# Diabetes — Clinical Reference Document

---

## 1. What Is Diabetes?

Diabetes mellitus is a chronic metabolic disease characterized by elevated blood glucose (blood sugar) levels, resulting from defects in insulin production, insulin action, or both. Insulin is a hormone produced by the pancreas that allows glucose from food to enter cells and be used for energy.

When the body cannot produce enough insulin or cannot use it effectively, glucose builds up in the bloodstream instead of being used by cells — leading to **hyperglycemia**, which over time causes serious damage to the heart, blood vessels, eyes, kidneys, and nerves.

---

## 2. Types of Diabetes

### 2.1 Type 1 Diabetes
- **Cause:** Autoimmune destruction of insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas
- **Onset:** Usually in childhood or young adulthood, but can occur at any age
- **Insulin dependence:** Always required — the body produces little to no insulin
- **Prevalence:** ~5–10% of all diabetes cases
- **Key characteristics:** Sudden onset, often with ketoacidosis; not linked to lifestyle

### 2.2 Type 2 Diabetes
- **Cause:** Insulin resistance combined with progressive insulin secretion failure
- **Onset:** Usually in adults over 45, but increasingly in younger people
- **Insulin dependence:** Not always required initially; managed with lifestyle and/or medication
- **Prevalence:** ~90–95% of all diabetes cases
- **Key characteristics:** Often asymptomatic for years; strongly linked to obesity, inactivity, and age

### 2.3 Gestational Diabetes
- **Cause:** Hormonal changes during pregnancy that cause insulin resistance
- **Onset:** Typically diagnosed in the 2nd or 3rd trimester
- **Resolution:** Usually resolves after delivery, but increases lifetime risk of Type 2
- **Risk to baby:** Macrosomia, preterm birth, neonatal hypoglycemia

### 2.4 Prediabetes
- Blood glucose is higher than normal but not yet in the diabetic range
- **HbA1c:** 5.7%–6.4% (normal < 5.7%, diabetic ≥ 6.5%)
- **Fasting glucose:** 100–125 mg/dL (normal < 100, diabetic ≥ 126)
- Strongly reversible through lifestyle modification

### 2.5 Other Types
- **MODY (Maturity-Onset Diabetes of the Young):** Rare genetic forms
- **Secondary Diabetes:** Caused by other conditions (e.g., pancreatitis, Cushing's syndrome)
- **LADA (Latent Autoimmune Diabetes in Adults):** Slowly progressing autoimmune form

## 3. Risk Factors

### Non-Modifiable
| Factor | Details |
|---|---|
| **Age** | Risk increases significantly after age 45; highest in seniors (60+) |
| **Family History** | First-degree relative with diabetes doubles the risk |
| **Ethnicity** | Higher prevalence in African, Hispanic, Asian, and Indigenous populations |
| **Genetics** | Specific gene variants (e.g., HLA alleles in Type 1) |

### Modifiable
| Factor | Details |
|---|---|
| **Obesity / Overweight** | BMI ≥ 25 significantly increases insulin resistance |
| **Physical Inactivity** | Sedentary lifestyle reduces insulin sensitivity |
| **Unhealthy Diet** | High sugar, refined carbs, and processed food intake |
| **Smoking** | Increases insulin resistance and beta-cell damage |
| **Hypertension** | Blood pressure ≥ 140/90 mmHg is strongly associated |
| **Dyslipidemia** | Abnormal cholesterol / triglyceride levels |
| **Sleep Disorders** | Sleep apnea and chronic sleep deprivation raise glucose |

### Comorbidities Associated with Diabetes
- **Hypertension** — very common co-occurrence (metabolic syndrome)
- **Heart Disease** — shared cardiovascular risk pathway
- **Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)** — associated with insulin resistance in women
- **Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD)**

## 4. Clinical Diagnostic Criteria

Diabetes is diagnosed by any one of the following (confirmed with a repeat test):

| Test | Normal | Prediabetes | Diabetes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HbA1c** | < 5.7% | 5.7%–6.4% | ≥ 6.5% |
| **Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG)** | < 100 mg/dL | 100–125 mg/dL | ≥ 126 mg/dL |
| **2-Hour Plasma Glucose (OGTT)** | < 140 mg/dL | 140–199 mg/dL | ≥ 200 mg/dL |
| **Random Plasma Glucose** | — | — | ≥ 200 mg/dL + symptoms |

### Key Biomarkers Explained

#### HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin)
- Reflects **average blood sugar over the past 2–3 months**
- Not affected by recent meals — most reliable for long-term control
- Clinical gold standard for both diagnosis and monitoring
- Each 1% reduction in HbA1c reduces complications risk by ~20–30%

#### Blood Glucose Level
- Fasting glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL is diagnostic of diabetes
- Postprandial (after meal) glucose > 200 mg/dL is a strong indicator
- Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) now available for real-time tracking

#### BMI (Body Mass Index)
| BMI Range | Category |
|---|---|
| < 18.5 | Underweight |
| 18.5 – 24.9 | Healthy |
| 25.0 – 29.9 | Overweight |
| 30.0 – 39.9 | Obese |
| ≥ 40.0 | Extremely Obese |

BMI ≥ 30 is associated with significantly higher diabetes risk due to increased visceral fat and insulin resistance.

## 5. Symptoms

### Common Symptoms
- Frequent urination (polyuria)
- Excessive thirst (polydipsia)
- Unexplained weight loss
- Extreme fatigue
- Blurred vision
- Slow-healing wounds or frequent infections
- Tingling or numbness in hands/feet (neuropathy sign)

### Type 1 Specific
- Rapid onset of symptoms
- Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA): nausea, vomiting, fruity breath, rapid breathing

### Type 2 Specific
- Often **asymptomatic** for years
- Gradually worsening symptoms
- Skin darkening in body folds (acanthosis nigricans)

## 6. Complications

### Short-Term (Acute)
| Complication | Description |
|---|---|
| **Hypoglycemia** | Blood glucose < 70 mg/dL; dizziness, confusion, fainting |
| **Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)** | Mostly Type 1; life-threatening buildup of ketones |
| **Hyperglycemic Hyperosmolar State (HHS)** | Mostly Type 2; severely high glucose, dehydration |

### Long-Term (Chronic)
| System | Complication |
|---|---|
| **Eyes** | Diabetic retinopathy → blindness |
| **Kidneys** | Diabetic nephropathy → chronic kidney disease |
| **Nerves** | Diabetic neuropathy → pain, numbness, amputations |
| **Heart & Vessels** | 2–4× higher risk of cardiovascular disease and stroke |
| **Feet** | Diabetic foot ulcers; leading cause of non-traumatic amputation |
| **Immune System** | Higher susceptibility to infections |

## 7. Treatment & Management

### Lifestyle Interventions (First Line for Type 2 & Prediabetes)
- **Diet:** Low glycemic index foods, reduced refined carbohydrates, portion control
- **Exercise:** 150+ minutes/week of moderate aerobic activity; resistance training
- **Weight loss:** Even 5–10% body weight reduction significantly improves insulin sensitivity
- **Smoking cessation:** Reduces cardiovascular and metabolic risk
- **Sleep hygiene:** 7–9 hours per night

### Medications

#### Oral Medications (Type 2)
| Drug Class | Example | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Biguanides | Metformin | Reduces hepatic glucose production |
| SGLT-2 Inhibitors | Empagliflozin | Promotes glucose excretion via urine |
| GLP-1 Agonists | Semaglutide | Stimulates insulin, reduces appetite |
| DPP-4 Inhibitors | Sitagliptin | Enhances incretin effect |
| Sulfonylureas | Glibenclamide | Stimulates insulin secretion |

#### Insulin Therapy
| Type | Onset | Duration | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rapid-acting | 15 min | 3–5 hrs | Before meals |
| Short-acting | 30–60 min | 6–8 hrs | Before meals |
| Intermediate-acting | 1–2 hrs | 12–18 hrs | Twice daily |
| Long-acting | 1–2 hrs | 20–24 hrs | Once daily basal |

### Blood Glucose Monitoring
- **Self-monitoring:** Finger-prick glucose meters
- **Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGM):** Real-time glucose tracking
- **Target range:** 80–130 mg/dL fasting; < 180 mg/dL postprandial (general guidelines)

---

## 8. Prevention

### Primary Prevention (Before Onset)
- Maintain healthy weight (BMI < 25)
- Regular physical activity
- Balanced diet rich in fiber, vegetables, lean proteins
- Avoid excessive sugar and processed foods
- Regular screening if at-risk (age > 45, BMI ≥ 25, family history)

### Secondary Prevention (Slowing Progression)
- Strict glycemic control (target HbA1c < 7%)
- Blood pressure management (< 130/80 mmHg)
- Lipid management (LDL < 100 mg/dL)
- Annual eye, kidney, and foot examinations
- Vaccinations (influenza, pneumococcal)

---

## 9. Smoking and Diabetes

Smoking has a well-documented bidirectional relationship with diabetes:

- **Insulin resistance:** Nicotine and other compounds impair insulin signaling
- **Beta-cell damage:** Oxidative stress from smoking damages insulin-producing cells
- **Abdominal fat:** Smoking promotes visceral fat accumulation
- **Cardiovascular amplification:** Smokers with diabetes have dramatically higher heart disease risk
- **Former smokers** remain at elevated risk for several years after quitting

---

## 10. Age and Diabetes Risk

| Age Group | Risk Profile |
|---|---|
| **Children (< 12)** | Mainly Type 1; Type 2 rare but rising due to childhood obesity |
| **Teens (12–17)** | Increasing Type 2 incidence; Type 1 peak onset period |
| **Young Adults (18–34)** | Low risk unless obese or family history present |
| **Middle Aged (35–59)** | Risk increases with lifestyle factors and weight gain |
| **Seniors (60+)** | Highest prevalence; often with multiple comorbidities |

---

## 11. Gender Differences

- **Men** are diagnosed at a lower BMI threshold due to more visceral fat storage
- **Women** face unique risk during pregnancy (gestational diabetes)
- **Postmenopausal women** experience increased risk due to hormonal changes
- **PCOS** affects ~10% of reproductive-age women and significantly raises diabetes risk

## 12. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **HbA1c** | Glycated hemoglobin; reflects 3-month average blood glucose |
| **Insulin** | Pancreatic hormone that enables glucose uptake into cells |
| **Glucose** | Primary blood sugar; the body's main energy source |
| **Hyperglycemia** | Abnormally high blood glucose |
| **Hypoglycemia** | Abnormally low blood glucose (< 70 mg/dL) |
| **Insulin resistance** | Cells fail to respond normally to insulin |
| **Beta cells** | Pancreatic cells that produce insulin |
| **SMOTE** | Synthetic Minority Oversampling Technique (used in ML to handle class imbalance) |
| **BMI** | Body Mass Index; weight (kg) / height² (m²) |
| **DKA** | Diabetic Ketoacidosis; life-threatening metabolic emergency |
| **CGM** | Continuous Glucose Monitor |
| **OGTT** | Oral Glucose Tolerance Test |
| **Prediabetes** | HbA1c 5.7–6.4%; intermediate glucose state |
| **Metformin** | First-line oral medication for Type 2 diabetes |
